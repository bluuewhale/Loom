- Constants: `goroutines = 4`, `iterations = 3`.
- Use `sync.WaitGroup` (already imported).

  1. Build its own `g := buildGraph(testdata.KarateClubEdges)` â€” distinct graph per goroutine (critical: Graph is NOT safe for concurrent writes).
  2. Create its own `det := NewOnlineEgoSplitting(EgoSplittingOptions{LocalDetector: NewLouvain(LouvainOptions{Seed: int64(idx + 1)}), GlobalDetector: NewLouvain(LouvainOptions{Seed: int64(idx + 1)})})` â€” distinct detector per goroutine.
  3. Call `prior, err := det.Detect(g)` to get initial result.
  4. Loop `iterations` times (j=0..2):
     - `newNode := NodeID(34 + j)`
     - `g.AddNode(newNode, 1.0)`
     - `g.AddEdge(0, newNode, 1.0)`
     - Build `delta := GraphDelta{AddedNodes: []NodeID{newNode}, AddedEdges: []DeltaEdge{{From: 0, To: newNode, Weight: 1.0}}}`

- `wg.Wait()` after all goroutines launched.

**Anti-patterns to avoid (from research):**
- Do NOT add any timing/speedup assertions â€” race detector adds 3x overhead making timing flaky.
- Do NOT share a single `*Graph` or single detector across goroutines.
- Do NOT attempt to write to `prior` after passing it to `Update()`.
