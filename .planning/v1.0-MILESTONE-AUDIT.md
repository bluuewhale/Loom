### Integration Gap: idxBuf Not Threaded Through Leiden's louvainState Wrapper

**Impact:** Performance-only, no correctness issue.

`leiden.go` constructs an ephemeral `louvainState` wrapper for each `phase1` call. The wrapper includes `partition`, `commStr`, `neighborBuf`, `neighborDirty`, `candidateBuf`, and `rng` — but not `idxBuf`. Since `leidenState` has no `idxBuf` field and the wrapper starts with nil `idxBuf`, `phase1` allocates `make([]int32, n)` on every Leiden phase1 call instead of reusing a pooled buffer.

**Fix:** Add `idxBuf []int32` to `leidenState`, initialize with `make([]int32, 0, 128)` in pool constructor, thread into wrapper at leiden.go line ~146, and copy back after call.

**Cost without fix:** One `make([]int32, n)` per supergraph pass per Leiden Detect() call. Minor relative to eliminated allocations in Phases 2–3.
